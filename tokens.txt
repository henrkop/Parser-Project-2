These are token names for each of the tokens mentioned in the
grammar, checker.py contains most of it.

'IDENTIFIER'    - A variable or function name, e.g., x, my_var
'NUMBER'        - An decimal integer, e.g., 10, 250
'HEXNUMBER'     - An hexademical integer, e.g. 0xa, 0x13
'BOOL'          - A boolean, i.e. True, False
'TYPE'          - The type for the varibale, i.e. 'int' or 'hex'
'IF'            - The 'if' keyword for conditional statements
'ELSE'          - The 'else' keyword for conditional statements
'ENDIF'         - The 'endif' keyword for ending current block of if statement
'FOR'           - The 'for' keyword for loops
'TO'            - The 'to' keyword used in for loops
'ENDFOR'        - The 'endfor' keyword for ending current block of for statement
'PRINT'         - The 'print' keyword for output
'AND'           - The logical 'and' operator
'OR'            - The logical 'or' operator
'NOT'           - The logical 'not' operator
'PLUS'          - The addition operator: +
'MINUS'         - The subtraction or unary minus operator: -
'MULTIPLY'      - The multiplication operator: *
'DIVIDE'        - The division operator: /
'MODULO'        - The modulo operator: %
'EQ'            - The equality operator: ==
'NEQ'           - The inequality operator: !=
'LESS'          - The less than operator: <
'GREATER'       - The greater than operator: >
'EQUALS'        - The assignment operator: =
'LPAREN'        - Left parenthesis: (
'RPAREN'        - Right parenthesis: )
'COMMA'         - The comma separator for arguments: ,
'COLON'         - The colon used to start a block: :
'EOF'           - End of File marker
